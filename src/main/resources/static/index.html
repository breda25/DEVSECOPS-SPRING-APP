The 2026 Iran War (also known as the Third Persian Gulf War) is an ongoing major armed conflict primarily between a United States-led coalition alongside Israel against the Islamic Republic of Iran and its allied proxy network, commonly referred to as the Axis of Resistance. The war officially began on 28 February 2026 following a series of pre-emptive, decapitation strikes against Iranian military and political leadership targets.

Tensions in the region had been steadily escalating following the conclusion of the 2023–2025 regional crisis, which saw the normalization of limited cross-border skirmishes and drone warfare. Following intelligence reports indicating the imminent deployment of a nuclear device by the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), a joint military operation codenamed Epic Fury (United States) and Roaring Lion (Israel) initiated a series of coordinated strikes across Tehran and underground enrichment facilities.

Explosions visible over Tehran during the opening hours of Operation Epic Fury.

Iran responded with Operation True Promise IV, launching an unprecedented barrage of over 285 ballistic missiles and 1,500 drones targeting Israeli infrastructure, as well as US regional bases in Bahrain, Kuwait, and the United Arab Emirates. The conflict rapidly expanded to include full mobilization of Iranian proxy groups across Iraq, Syria, Lebanon, and Yemen, leading to the closure of the Strait of Hormuz and a subsequent global economic shock.

Background

The strategic environment of the Middle East had deteriorated significantly since 2024. Despite international diplomatic efforts, the enrichment of uranium at the Fordow Fuel Enrichment Plant had allegedly reached weapons-grade (90%) by late 2025. On 15 February 2026, the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) reported an "unexplained absence" of monitoring equipment at several key sites.

Opening Operations

Operations Epic Fury & Roaring Lion

At 02:45 IRST on 28 February 2026, the United States and Israel launched a massive, synchronized assault. Operation Roaring Lion focused on the suppression of enemy air defenses (SEAD), utilizing F-35I Adir stealth fighters to neutralize Iranian S-300 and domestically produced Bavar-373 anti-aircraft systems. Concurrently, Operation Epic Fury involved US B-2 Spirit bombers deploying GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrators (MOPs) against deeply buried nuclear facilities in Natanz and Fordow.

The strikes also targeted command and control centers in Tehran, reportedly resulting in the deaths of several high-ranking IRGC commanders. A cyberattack attributed to Unit 8200 paralyzed Iran's national power grid for 48 hours, plunging the capital into darkness during the bombardment.

Retaliation Phase

Operation True Promise IV

IRGC forces launching a Fattah-2 hypersonic missile during Operation True Promise IV.

In response to the decapitation strikes, Iran authorized Operation True Promise IV. Between 1 March and 3 March 2026, the IRGC Aerospace Force launched over 285 medium-range ballistic missiles (including the hypersonic Fattah-2) and swarms of over 1,500 Shahed-136 and Shahed-238 loitering munitions.

The targets included:

  • Nevatim Airbase and Ramon Airbase in Israel.
  • Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar (partially intercepted).
  • Naval Support Activity Bahrain, headquarters of the US Fifth Fleet.

While the Arrow 3, David's Sling, and US Patriot missile defense systems achieved a reported 82% interception rate, several hypersonic missiles breached the defense shield, causing significant damage to the Dimona radar array and port facilities in Haifa. Synchronized attacks by Hezbollah from Lebanon and the Houthis from Yemen stretched coalition air defenses to their limits.

Global Economic Impact

On 4 March 2026, the IRGC Navy officially announced the mining and closure of the Strait of Hormuz, blocking roughly 20% of global oil consumption. Within 24 hours, the price of Brent crude surged past $180 per barrel, triggering massive sell-offs in global stock markets. The United States announced the release of 150 million barrels from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve, though market analysts suggest this will only offset the shortage for approximately three weeks.
